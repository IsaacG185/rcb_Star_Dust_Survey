History:
- First discovered in 1795 (Pigott & Englefield 1797).

R Coronae Borealis Variables: 
- Also known as RCB, R CrB, R Coronae Borealis stars, and R Coronae Borealis variables.

- Hydrogen deficient and Carbon rich supergiants (Clayton 2012a):
    - Spectral Class/Type : F or G supergiants (Clayton 1996)
    - Effective Temperature : 5000 - 7000 k (Clayton 1996)

- Extremely rare stars; about 162 are known (Crawford et al. 2025), 
and it is believed that there are only about 300 - 500 in the Milky Way Galaxy (Crawford et al. 2020).

- RCB stars undergo massive declines of up to 8 magnitudes caused by the formation of 
carbon dust at irregular intervals (Clayton 2012b).
